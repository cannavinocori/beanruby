I have always known Bean Ruby was a genius.

When I met Bean’s mom 9 years ago, she told me that their family comes from a long maternal line of artists, and that each generation passes down traces of their work to the next, that it’s held in their bodies. She told me they can feel it when they draw a line, can feel the hand of someone else, someone present. I think Bean’s art is about a line.

Bean used a line to teach me about the world. She showed me that a line is active, that it nets out into a grid, that it holds memory.

Bean showed me the threads that create digital space, how they work, where they fray. She gifted me with an investment in how we archive  and read (in person and online) craft. Bean makes me believe that we have another opportunity, to appreciate and discern types of meaning that get cast away, compartmentalized.

Her vision is ever present with me. Bean helps me see the hand embroideries on my grandmother’s pillowcases and remember that I can use my computer to make things I love. She taught me to turn the stitched fabric around, inside out, that sometimes the most beautiful part is the back. With its tangles of knots and effort to hold.

Knowing and loving Bean has opened my life up to a type of beauty and symmetry and chaos that I couldn’t have seen. Her art, her life, her textile practice do that to people, and that is why I believe, your program would be lucky to have her.

Cori
